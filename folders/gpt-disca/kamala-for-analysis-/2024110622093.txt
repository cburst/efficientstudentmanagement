Dowd, a 22-year-old senior at Howard who voted for Ms. Harris, sat and watched the crowd, reflecting on what Howard taught women like her and Ms. Harris, namely 'being yourself, being Black and going into the world being proud of that.' 'I don't know what the future holds at this point,' Ms. Dowd said. 'I think the world is scared of a woman leading the country, let alone a Black woman. That's just what we face. That's where history has led us to now.' Katie Rogers contributed reporting from New York and Zolan Kanno-Youngs from Washington.
